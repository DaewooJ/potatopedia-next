Danespo Potato Varieties — Official Breeder Data
Source: Danespo A/S (Denmark), www.danespo.com
Danespo is the leading potato breeder in Northern Europe, specializing in varieties for consumption, processing, and starch production.

Variety: Folva
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Medium early
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow to yellow
Tuber Shape: Oval
Cooking Type: B (waxy type)
Primary Use: Consumption and frying
Dry Matter: Medium to high
Yield: Very high yield potential
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Ro1, Ro2, Ro3, Ro4, Ro5. Wart disease: resistant. Foliage blight: good. Tuber blight: good. Common scab: very good.
Storage: Long-term cold storage recommended. Medium dormancy.
Special Features: Easy to grow with high and rapid foliage covering. Well-shaped yellow tubers. Exceptionally broad nematode resistance (Ro1-5).

Variety: Saturna
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Medium late
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow
Tuber Shape: Round to round oval
Cooking Type: BC (waxy to mealy)
Primary Use: Crisp/chip production
Dry Matter: Medium (suited for crisps)
Yield: Medium to high
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Ro1. Wart disease: type 1. Foliage blight: average. Tuber blight: good. Common scab: good.
Storage: Long, good storability with extended dormancy
Special Features: Standard variety for crisp production in Northern Europe. Requires high nitrogen and sufficient water supply. Classic chipping potato.

Variety: Sarpo Mira
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark) / originally bred in Hungary
Maturity: Late
Skin Color: Red
Flesh Color: Pale yellow
Tuber Shape: Long
Cooking Type: B (waxy)
Primary Use: Consumption and French fries
Dry Matter: Medium to high
Yield: High yield of big potatoes
Disease Resistance: Late blight foliage: VERY GOOD (probably the best late blight resistance on the market). Tuber blight: good. Wart disease: type 1. Common scab: rather susceptible. Nematodes: susceptible.
Storage: Long, very good storability
Special Features: Allows cultivation with greatly reduced chemical/fungicide input. Outstanding late blight resistance makes it the premier variety for organic and low-spray farming. High, erect foliage.

Variety: Ballerina
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Early to medium early
Skin Color: Yellow, beautiful skin
Flesh Color: Yellow
Tuber Shape: Oval
Cooking Type: AB (firm cooking)
Primary Use: Consumption/salad, washable
Dry Matter: Medium
Yield: High
Disease Resistance: Wart disease: type 1. Tuber blight: good. Common scab: good. Foliage blight: average. Nematodes: susceptible.
Storage: Long, very good storability
Special Features: 12-15 tubers per plant. Highly washable with beautiful skin finish. Suitable for better soils. Slow emergence. Premium retail variety.

Variety: Solist
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Extremely early
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Yellow
Tuber Shape: Round to oval
Cooking Type: AB (firm cooking)
Primary Use: Fresh, direct from field or storage, washable
Dry Matter: Medium
Yield: High early yield
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Ro1, Ro4. Foliage blight: medium (early varieties always susceptible). Tuber blight: medium. Common scab: good. Wart disease: susceptible.
Storage: Medium to long dormancy
Special Features: Probably the earliest yellow-flesh variety on the market. 10-12 tubers per plant. Requires low nitrogen. Premium early-season retail variety.

Variety: Royal
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Medium late
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow
Tuber Shape: Big oval, rather square
Cooking Type: BC
Primary Use: French fries, crisps, flakes
Dry Matter: High
Yield: Very high yield potential
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Ro1, Ro4. Wart disease: types 1, 2, 6. Foliage blight: fairly good. Tuber blight: fairly good. Common scab: good.
Storage: Long, very good storability
Special Features: Good drought resistance. Suitable for both sandy and clay soils. Low to medium nitrogen required. Multi-purpose processing variety.

Variety: Wotan
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Late
Skin Color: Red
Flesh Color: Cream
Tuber Shape: Round to round-oval
Cooking Type: CD (floury to very floury)
Primary Use: Starch production
Dry Matter: Very high (23-25%)
Yield: High
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Ro1. Wart disease: type 1. Foliage blight: high. Tuber blight: high. PVY: high. Common scab: medium.
Storage: Medium dormancy
Special Features: Strong and covering foliage. 10-13 tubers per plant. High dry matter content (23-25%) makes it exceptionally suited for industrial starch extraction. Outstanding virus Y resistance.

Variety: Hermes
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark)
Maturity: Medium early to medium late
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow to yellow
Tuber Shape: Round to round oval
Cooking Type: BC (fairly firm to mealy)
Primary Use: Crisp production
Dry Matter: High
Yield: Moderate to high
Disease Resistance: Nematodes: Pa3. Foliage blight: average. Tuber blight: rather good. Common scab: average. Wart disease: susceptible.
Storage: Long, good storability
Special Features: Standard variety for crisp production. Medium high and covering foliage. Good long-term storage maintaining processing quality.

Variety: Bintje
Breeder: Danespo (Denmark) / originally bred in Netherlands, 1910
Maturity: Medium late
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Yellow
Tuber Shape: Long oval
Cooking Type: B-C
Primary Use: French fries, table
Dry Matter: Medium-high
Yield: Medium to high
Disease Resistance: Late blight: susceptible. Common scab: susceptible. Nematodes: susceptible.
Storage: Good
Special Features: One of the most historically important potato varieties in the world, bred in 1910. The original Belgian frite potato. Still grown for its distinctive frying quality and flavor despite susceptibility to diseases. Cultural heritage variety.

Variety: Spunta (Danespo)
Breeder: Originally HZPC, marketed by multiple companies including Danespo
Maturity: Medium early
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow
Tuber Shape: Long
Cooking Type: B
Primary Use: Consumption, fresh market
Dry Matter: Medium
Yield: High
Disease Resistance: Broad adaptation. Wart disease: resistant type 1.
Storage: Good
Special Features: Extremely popular in North Africa, Mediterranean, and Middle East markets. Excellent heat and drought tolerance. One of the most widely grown varieties in developing countries.
