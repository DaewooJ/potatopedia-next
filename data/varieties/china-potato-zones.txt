China's Four Potato Agro-Ecological Zones — Structured Knowledge File
Source: Hu et al. (2022), "Phenotypic variability and genetic diversity analysis of cultivated potatoes in China," Frontiers in Plant Science, PMC9541749; FAOSTAT 2023; CIP Annual Reports; Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences (CAAS)

China is the world's largest potato producer, growing approximately 94.4 million tonnes on 4.7 million hectares (FAOSTAT 2023). Chinese potato production is organized across four distinct agro-ecological zones, each with different growing seasons, variety requirements, and end-use markets. This classification was established by Chinese agricultural authorities and is referenced in peer-reviewed literature (Hu et al., 2022, Frontiers in Plant Science).

Zone 1: Northern Single-Cropping Zone
Provinces: Inner Mongolia, Gansu, Ningxia, Qinghai, Heilongjiang, Jilin, Hebei (northern), Shanxi, Shaanxi (northern)
Growing Season: Single crop per year (planted April-May, harvested September-October)
Altitude: 1,000-3,000 meters above sea level
Climate: Continental climate with cold winters and short growing seasons
Variety Requirements: Late-maturing varieties preferred (100-130+ day growth period). High starch content for processing. Drought tolerance critical in arid Northwest regions.
Key Varieties: Longshu series (Gansu), Qingshu series (Qinghai), Kexin series (Heilongjiang), Jizhangshu series (Hebei)
Importance: Largest zone by production volume. Produces the majority of China's starch and processing potatoes. Inner Mongolia, Gansu, and Heilongjiang are among the top producing provinces.
Challenges: Drought stress (especially Gansu, Ningxia), short frost-free period, limited certified seed supply in remote areas.

Zone 2: Southwestern Mixed-Cropping Zone
Provinces: Yunnan, Sichuan, Guizhou, Chongqing, parts of Hubei and Hunan
Growing Season: Multiple planting seasons possible due to altitude variation. Spring crop (Feb-Mar planting), autumn crop (Aug-Sep planting), and winter crop at lower elevations.
Altitude: Highly variable — 500 to 3,500 meters above sea level
Climate: Subtropical to temperate depending on altitude. High humidity and rainfall.
Variety Requirements: Late blight resistance is critical due to high humidity. Medium to late maturity. Multi-purpose varieties for table and processing.
Key Varieties: Cooperation 88 (dominant in Yunnan), Chuanyu series (Sichuan), Mira, Qingshu 9
Importance: Second largest zone. Potatoes are a food security staple in mountainous areas where rice cannot grow. Highest diversity of growing conditions within a single zone.
Challenges: Late blight (Phytophthora infestans) is the most devastating disease. Difficult terrain limits mechanization. Small-scale farming predominates.

Zone 3: Central Plain Double-Cropping Zone
Provinces: Shandong, Henan, Jiangsu, Anhui, Hubei (eastern), Hebei (southern)
Growing Season: Two potato crops per year. Spring crop (Feb-Mar planting, June harvest) and autumn crop (Aug planting, Nov harvest).
Altitude: Low elevation (under 500 meters)
Climate: Warm temperate to subtropical. Hot summers unsuitable for potato growth.
Variety Requirements: Early maturity essential (60-90 day growth period). Yellow flesh preferred for fresh market. Heat tolerance increasingly important.
Key Varieties: Favorita (Dutch import, extremely popular), Zhongshu series (CAAS), early-maturing European imports
Importance: Growing zone focused on fresh market supply to major cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Wuhan). Double-cropping maximizes land use in densely populated plains.
Challenges: Heat stress during summer limits potato to spring and autumn windows. Competition with other crops for land.

Zone 4: Southern Winter-Cropping Zone
Provinces: Guangdong, Guangxi, Fujian, parts of Zhejiang and Jiangxi
Growing Season: Winter crop only — planted October-November, harvested March-May. Potatoes grow during the mild winter when temperatures are suitable (10-25 degrees Celsius).
Altitude: Low elevation (under 300 meters)
Climate: Subtropical to tropical. Only suitable for potatoes during cooler winter months.
Variety Requirements: Very early maturity (55-75 day growth period). Yellow flesh and attractive appearance for urban fresh markets. Exported to neighboring countries.
Key Varieties: Favorita, early-maturing Dutch and European imports, Zhongshu 3
Importance: Rapidly expanding zone, supplying fresh potatoes to urban markets in southern China and for export to Southeast Asia. Winter cropping takes advantage of otherwise idle farmland after rice harvest.
Challenges: Short growing window, high disease pressure from warm humid conditions, limited cold storage infrastructure.

Cross-Zone Notes:
- China's total potato area is approximately 4.7 million hectares (FAOSTAT 2023), making it the largest in the world.
- Average national yield is 20.1 tonnes per hectare (FAOSTAT 2023), significantly below Western leaders (Netherlands 43.6, USA 51.0 t/ha).
- Only an estimated 20-30% of China's potato area uses certified seed (CIP estimates), compared to nearly 100% in Netherlands and USA.
- The Chinese Ministry of Agriculture launched the "Potato as Fourth Staple" strategy in 2015, promoting potato alongside rice, wheat, and corn.
- CIP (International Potato Center) has collaborated with Chinese breeding institutions since the 1980s, contributing germplasm that led to varieties like Qingshu 9 and Cooperation 88.
