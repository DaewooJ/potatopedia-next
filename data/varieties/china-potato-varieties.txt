Chinese Potato Varieties — Structured Knowledge File
Sources: Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science, PMC9541749 (149 Chinese varieties study); Wang et al. (2019), Frontiers in Plant Science, PMC6393402 (292 genotypes study); RTB/CGIAR Qingshu 9 case study; CABI Agriculture and Bioscience, "The curious case of C-88" (2020); FAOSTAT 2023; CIP Annual Reports; Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences (CAAS) variety registration data; Provincial Academy variety release records.

Note on data: Chinese potato variety data comes from national variety registration records, peer-reviewed studies, and CIP publications. Where specific numerical values (dry matter, starch content, growth period) are cited, they derive from the Hu et al. (2022) study which compiled phenotypic data from 149 registered Chinese varieties, or from individual variety publications. The study reports that across 149 Chinese varieties, growth periods range from 50-144 days (average 97.38 days), dry matter ranges from 14.8-28.2% (average 21.76%), starch content ranges from 10.3-22.86% (average 15.49%), protein ranges from 0.53-3.52% (average 2.16%), and vitamin C ranges from 8.93-48.0 mg/100g (average 17.50 mg/100g).

=== TIER 1 VARIETIES (Most Widely Grown) ===

Variety: Qingshu 9 (青薯9号)
Chinese Name: 青薯9号
Breeder: Qinghai Academy of Agriculture and Forestry Sciences (with CIP germplasm)
CIP Connection: Derived from CIP clone 392797.22, cross of CIP 387521.3 x Aphrodite. Known internationally as UNICA (first released in Peru in 1998).
Year Released: 2006 (Qinghai provincial), 2011 (national approval in China)
Growth Period: Medium late (approximately 110-120 days)
Tuber Shape: Oval
Skin Color: Red
Flesh Color: Yellow
Dry Matter: High (above average for Chinese varieties)
Starch Content: Medium-high
Primary Use: Fresh consumption and French fry processing
Disease Resistance: Resistant to viruses, tolerant to drought, stable yields across varied environments (RTB/CGIAR)
Growing Zones: Northern single-cropping zone and Southwestern mixed-cropping zone. Grown in 13+ major potato-producing provinces including Qinghai, Ningxia, Gansu, Inner Mongolia, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Sichuan.
Area Planted: Over 150,000 hectares in China (as of 2015, RTB/CGIAR data)
Production: Approximately 4.5 million tonnes annually (2015 estimate)
Yield: Approximately 30 tonnes/hectare (versus national average of 20 t/ha)
Key Features: China's most widely planted potato variety as of 2015. Introduced to China by CIP in 2001. Adapted to altitudes from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Also introduced to Kenya, Rwanda, Ethiopia, Vietnam, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, and Bangladesh. Represents the most successful CIP-to-China technology transfer in potato breeding.
Source: RTB/CGIAR, "Improved potato variety Qingshu 9: A success story," CIP publications.

Variety: Cooperation 88 / Hezuo 88 (合作88)
Chinese Name: 合作88
Breeder: Yunnan Normal University in collaboration with CIP (International Potato Center)
Year Released: 1996 (Yunnan provincial approval)
Growth Period: Late (approximately 120-130 days)
Tuber Shape: Oval to round-oval
Skin Color: Yellow with pink/red eyes
Flesh Color: Light yellow
Dry Matter: Medium (approximately 19-21%)
Starch Content: Medium (approximately 13-15%)
Primary Use: Table/fresh consumption, multi-purpose
Disease Resistance: Good late blight resistance (critical for Yunnan's humid conditions), moderate virus resistance
Growing Zones: Southwestern mixed-cropping zone — dominant variety in Yunnan, Guizhou, and parts of Sichuan
Area Planted: One of the most widely grown varieties in Southwest China, covering hundreds of thousands of hectares
Yield: Good yield potential, approximately 25-35 t/ha depending on altitude and conditions
Key Features: Developed through CIP-China collaboration using CIP germplasm crossed with local adapted varieties. Named "Cooperation" to reflect the international partnership. Dominant variety in Yunnan province for over two decades. Well-adapted to diverse altitude conditions (1,000-3,000 masl). Particularly important for food security in mountainous areas of Southwest China where rice cannot grow.
Source: CABI Agriculture and Bioscience, "The curious case of C-88" (2020); CIP Annual Reports.

Variety: Longshu 7 (陇薯7号)
Chinese Name: 陇薯7号
Breeder: Gansu Academy of Agricultural Sciences
Year Released: 2006
Growth Period: Late (approximately 120-130 days)
Tuber Shape: Round to round-oval
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Light yellow
Dry Matter: High (approximately 23-25%)
Starch Content: High (approximately 17-19%)
Primary Use: Starch production and table use
Disease Resistance: Good late blight resistance, moderate virus tolerance
Growing Zones: Northern single-cropping zone — primarily Gansu, Ningxia, Shaanxi
Yield: High, approximately 30-40 t/ha under good conditions
Key Features: Major starch variety in Northwest China. High dry matter makes it excellent for industrial starch extraction. Part of the Longshu series developed by Gansu Academy, which is one of China's most productive potato breeding programs. Well-adapted to the semi-arid conditions of the Loess Plateau.
Source: Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science; Gansu Academy variety registration records.

Variety: Zhongshu 3 (中薯3号)
Chinese Name: 中薯3号
Breeder: Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences (CAAS), Institute of Vegetables and Flowers, Beijing
Year Released: 2003
Growth Period: Early to medium early (approximately 70-85 days)
Tuber Shape: Oval
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Yellow
Dry Matter: Medium (approximately 18-20%)
Starch Content: Medium
Primary Use: Table/fresh market
Disease Resistance: Moderate general resistance
Growing Zones: Central Plain double-cropping zone and Southern winter-cropping zone — suited to short growing seasons
Yield: Good early yield
Key Features: Early maturity makes it suitable for China's double-cropping zones where potatoes must be harvested quickly before summer heat. Part of the Zhongshu series developed by CAAS, China's premier agricultural research institution. Yellow flesh and attractive appearance suited to Chinese urban fresh markets.
Source: Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science; CAAS variety registration records.

Variety: Favorita (费乌瑞它)
Chinese Name: 费乌瑞它
Breeder: Originally bred in the Netherlands; introduced to China
Year Released: Introduced to China in the 1980s-1990s
Growth Period: Early (approximately 65-80 days)
Tuber Shape: Oval
Skin Color: Yellow
Flesh Color: Deep yellow
Dry Matter: Low to medium (approximately 16-18%)
Starch Content: Low to medium
Primary Use: Fresh market/table (premium)
Disease Resistance: Moderate; susceptible to late blight
Growing Zones: Central Plain double-cropping zone and Southern winter-cropping zone — extremely popular in Shandong, Hubei, Guangdong, and Fujian
Yield: Good early yield
Key Features: One of the most popular imported varieties in China, especially in the double-cropping and winter-cropping zones. Deep yellow flesh is highly valued in Chinese fresh markets. Early maturity fits short growing windows. Dominant in the rapidly growing Southern winter-cropping zone where it is planted after rice harvest.
Source: Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science; CAAS variety registration records.

Variety: Atlantic (大西洋)
Chinese Name: 大西洋
Breeder: Originally bred by USDA (United States); introduced to China
Year Released: Introduced to China in the 1990s
Growth Period: Medium early (approximately 90-100 days)
Tuber Shape: Round
Skin Color: White to light yellow
Flesh Color: White
Dry Matter: High (approximately 22-24%)
Starch Content: Medium-high
Reducing Sugar: Very low
Primary Use: Chip/crisp production
Disease Resistance: Moderate; susceptible to late blight
Growing Zones: Northern single-cropping zone — primarily Inner Mongolia, Hebei, and other northern provinces with processing facilities
Yield: Medium
Key Features: The dominant potato chip variety in China. Introduced from the United States where it is also a leading chipstock variety. Low reducing sugar content produces excellent frying color for crisp/chip manufacturing. Used by PepsiCo (Lay's) and other major chip manufacturers in China. Required by most Chinese chip factories.
Source: USDA GRIN database; Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science.

Variety: Kexin 1 (克新1号)
Chinese Name: 克新1号
Breeder: Heilongjiang Academy of Agricultural Sciences (Keshan Potato Research Institute)
Year Released: 1980s
Growth Period: Medium to late (approximately 100-115 days)
Tuber Shape: Round to oval
Skin Color: White to light yellow
Flesh Color: White to light yellow
Dry Matter: Medium-high (approximately 20-22%)
Starch Content: Medium (approximately 14-16%)
Primary Use: Table and starch
Disease Resistance: Good cold tolerance, moderate disease resistance
Growing Zones: Northern single-cropping zone — Heilongjiang, Jilin, Inner Mongolia
Yield: Good
Key Features: One of the most important varieties in Northeast China. Part of the Kexin series developed at the Keshan station, which has been a center of Chinese potato breeding since the 1960s. Well-adapted to the cold climate and short growing season of Heilongjiang province. Reliable performance across Northeast China's potato belt.
Source: Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science; Heilongjiang Academy variety records.

Chinese Name: 川芋117
Breeder: Sichuan Academy of Agricultural Sciences, Crop Research Institute (CRI)

Tuber Shape: Oval to round

Variety: Shepody (夏波蒂)
Chinese Name: 夏波蒂
Breeder: Originally bred in Canada (Agriculture Canada); introduced to China
Year Released: Introduced to China in the 1990s
Growth Period: Medium early (approximately 90-100 days)
Tuber Shape: Long oval
Skin Color: White to light yellow
Flesh Color: White
Dry Matter: High (approximately 21-23%)
Starch Content: Medium-high
Primary Use: French fry processing
Disease Resistance: Moderate; susceptible to late blight
Growing Zones: Northern single-cropping zone — Inner Mongolia, Hebei, Gansu (near processing facilities)
Yield: Medium to high
Key Features: Major French fry processing variety in China, imported from Canada. Used by McDonald's and other QSR chains in China. High dry matter and elongated shape produce good fry quality. One of the first dedicated fry varieties introduced to China as the fast-food industry expanded in the 1990s-2000s.
Source: USDA FAS China reports; Hu et al. (2022), Frontiers in Plant Science.
